Is this a sunrise or sunset camera?

How should it calibrate?

Your phone becomes the measuring tool. Hold the phone upright and press the housing flat against its back — camera at the top — so both face the same way. The phone’s compass gives the heading and its tilt sensor checks the housing is upright. Markers on your phone’s camera view show where the sun sets — today, at the equinox, and at the solstices.

Swing until the Equinox marker crosses the center line — that’s where the sun sets on the equinox.

Now check the camera’s own view — the markers should sit over open sky:

aimed —° — do the markers sit over open sky?

The camera spots the sun in its own view. We know exactly where the sun is in the sky right now, so that one sighting tells us precisely which way the camera faces. The camera should sit roughly at its mounted position.

☀ finding the sun…

A window facing due west sees the sun set there on the equinox — and every other set of the year falls within the camera’s wide view. Set it on the sill and the camera fine-tunes its own aim on the next clear sunset.
